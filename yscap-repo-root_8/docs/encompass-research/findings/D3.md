_Research agent D3 · 2026-07-19 · YS Capital borrower portal (Node/Express + Postgres) ← Encompass Developer Connect (instance BE11397907), Phase 1 strictly read-only._

> **Not legal advice.** This is a research synthesis of public regulatory materials, vendor terms, and industry norms, plus a review of the current repo's data-handling posture. Final applicability calls (especially business-purpose vs consumer coverage, state licensing, and ICE contract interpretation) belong with counsel and the licensing/compliance owner.

## 1. Framing: what kind of data this is, and what kind of lender YS Cap is

Two facts drive everything below:

1. **The data being pulled is mortgage-loan-file data about identifiable individuals** — names, addresses, DOBs, SSNs (Encompass field 65 / borrower objects), income/asset data, credit data, property data. Under GLBA this is the textbook definition of **NPI (nonpublic personal information)**: "personally identifiable financial information" collected in connection with providing a financial product or service ([FTC GLBA Privacy Rule guide](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/how-comply-privacy-consumer-financial-information-rule-gramm-leach-bliley-act)).
2. **YS Cap originates business-purpose loans** (fix-and-flip / rental / bridge, frequently to LLCs with individual guarantors). Business-purpose status **narrows some obligations (TILA/RESPA) but does not remove data-security obligations**, and much of the data in the file (guarantor SSN/DOB, personal credit reports, personal financial statements) is still personal information under state breach-notification and privacy statutes regardless of loan purpose.

**Practical posture the industry converges on:** treat the whole Encompass loan file as GLBA-grade NPI and protect it uniformly, rather than litigating record-by-record whether a datum belongs to a "consumer." That is also the posture the portal already codifies — the schema header says exactly this: `db/schema.sql:11` ("SSN is encrypted at rest… PII access is audit-logged (GLBA)") and the audit table is labeled "AUDIT — every read/write of PII (GLBA Safeguards)" (`db/schema.sql:319-335`).

## 2. GLBA Safeguards Rule (FTC, 16 CFR Part 314) — the controlling federal security baseline

YS Cap, as a nonbank mortgage/finance company, is an FTC-supervised "financial institution" — the FTC's own examples explicitly include "mortgage lenders … finance companies, mortgage brokers" ([FTC Safeguards Rule business guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/ftc-safeguards-rule-what-your-business-needs-know); rule text at [16 CFR Part 314, eCFR](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-16/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-314)).

### 2.1 The nine required elements of the written information security program (§314.4)

| # | Element | Encompass-pull implication |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Designate a **Qualified Individual** to own the program | The Encompass integration must have a named security owner; QI reports to board/owner annually |
| 2 | Written **risk assessment** | The integration is a new data flow — it must be added to the risk assessment *before* go-live |
| 3 | **Safeguards**: access controls, **data inventory**, **encryption in transit and at rest**, secure development, **MFA for anyone accessing customer information**, secure disposal, change management, **logging of authorized user activity** | Core of the checklist in §12. Note the *data inventory* duty: examiners expect you to know exactly which Encompass fields land where |
| 4 | **Monitor/test**: continuous monitoring OR annual pen test + semiannual vulnerability scans | Include the new sync service and its DB in scope |
| 5 | Staff **security awareness training** | Train staff who get new Encompass-sourced views |
| 6 | **Oversee service providers** (contracts + periodic reassessment) | ICE is a service provider of YS Cap; conversely YS Cap's hosting (Render, per `render.yaml`) holds NPI and needs the same treatment |
| 7 | Keep the program **current** | Re-assess when Phase 2 (any write-back) is considered |
| 8 | Written **incident response plan** | Must cover an Encompass-credential compromise scenario |
| 9 | QI's **annual written report** to the board/governing body | Include integration status/incidents |

Key definitions/obligations relevant here (same sources):

- **Encryption** of customer information at rest and in transit is required; alternatives need written QI approval.
- **MFA** is required for "anyone accessing customer information" (in effect since June 9, 2023 — see [Corbado summary of the MFA mandate](https://www.corbado.com/blog/ftc-safeguards-rule-mfa-compliance)). The portal already ships TOTP MFA and hardened password policy (`src/lib/crypto.js:66-106, 163-200`) and staff-login security work exists (`db/084_staff_login_security.sql:3`).
- **Disposal**: customer information must be securely disposed of **no later than two years after the most recent use** to serve the customer, *unless* retention is required by law or a legitimate business need — see §7 (Retention).
- **Small-institution carve-out**: institutions holding data on **fewer than 5,000 consumers** are exempt from a few elements (written risk assessment, continuous monitoring/pen-testing, written IR plan, annual board report) — YS Cap should confirm its count, but a growing lender should build to the full rule anyway.

### 2.2 FTC breach notification amendment (effective May 13, 2024)

- A **"notification event"** = unauthorized acquisition of **unencrypted** customer information involving **≥500 consumers**.
- Must notify the **FTC as soon as possible and no later than 30 days after discovery**, via the FTC's online form (company name, dates, count, data types, summary). Sources: [FTC press release](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2023/10/ftc-amends-safeguards-rule-require-non-banking-financial-institutions-report-data-security-breaches), [FTC blog "notification requirement now in effect"](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/blog/2024/05/safeguards-rule-notification-requirement-now-effect), [Federal Register final rule](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2023/11/13/2023-24412/standards-for-safeguarding-customer-information).
- **Encryption is the lever**: the trigger is *unencrypted* information (or encrypted information where the key was also compromised). Encrypting the Encompass-sourced store at rest, with keys held outside the DB, materially reduces notification exposure. Same logic under most state laws (§9).

## 3. GLBA Privacy Rule nuance for a business-purpose lender

- GLBA's Privacy Rule protects **"consumers"** — individuals who obtain financial products **"primarily for personal, family, or household purposes"** ([FTC Privacy Rule guide](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/how-comply-privacy-consumer-financial-information-rule-gramm-leach-bliley-act); [FDIC exam manual VIII-1](https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-compliance-examination-manual/viii-1-gramm-leach-bliley-act-privacy-consumer-financial)). A pure business-purpose loan to an LLC arguably produces no GLBA "consumer," so privacy-notice/opt-out duties may not attach the way they do for a consumer mortgage shop.
- **However:** (a) individual **guarantors'** SSNs, DOBs, and credit reports are personal data under state breach and privacy statutes regardless; (b) **FCRA** applies whenever a consumer report is pulled on an individual (Xactus soft pulls are already in the workflow — `db/005_rtl_workflow.sql:82`); (c) state examiners applying the CSBS programs (§4) do not carve out business-purpose files; and (d) misclassifying purpose is a known enforcement trap (see [CFPB amicus on business-purpose loans, California Mortgage Association summary](https://www.californiamortgageassociation.org/2024/02/29/the-cfpbs-recent-amicus-brief-on-business-purpose-loans-what-you-need-to-know/)).
- **Norm for private lenders:** run the Safeguards-Rule program as if all borrower/guarantor data were NPI; treat the Privacy-Rule notice/opt-out question as a separate counsel-level determination.

### Business-purpose vs consumer-mortgage obligations at a glance

| Regime | Consumer mortgage | Business-purpose (YS Cap) | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| TILA / Reg Z | Applies (LE/CD, ATR, HPML…) | **Exempt** under [12 CFR 1026.3(a)](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/rules-policy/regulations/1026/3/) — credit primarily for business/commercial purpose; non-owner-occupied rental property is deemed business purpose ([Doss Law overview](https://www.dosslaw.com/doss-guides/business-purpose-exemption-simplified/)) | Purpose must be documented per-loan (use-of-funds letter, occupancy cert); >14-day owner occupancy defeats the rental rule |
| RESPA / Reg X | Applies | **Exempt** for business-purpose loans ([12 CFR 1024.5](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/rules-policy/regulations/1024/5/)) | Same documentation caveat |
| ECOA / Reg B | Applies | **Still applies** (ECOA covers business credit; adverse-action + record-retention rules differ) | See §7 for the 25-month vs 12-month split |
| FCRA | Applies | **Applies** to consumer reports on individuals/guarantors | Permissible-purpose + adverse-action duties |
| GLBA Safeguards | Applies | **Treat as applying** (FTC "financial institution" status doesn't hinge on loan purpose; guarantor data is in scope as a practical matter) | §2 |
| GLBA Privacy notices | Applies | Counsel question (no "consumer" in a pure entity loan) | §3 |
| State breach-notification laws | Apply | **Apply** — keyed to the individual's data, not loan purpose | §9 |
| State licensing/exam (CSBS, NMLS) | Apply | **Apply where licensed** — several states license/examine business-purpose lenders secured by residential real estate | §4 |

## 4. State mortgage regulator exam expectations for data security

State regulators (coordinated through CSBS) examine nonbank lenders' IT/cyber posture with standardized work programs:

- **[Baseline Nonbank Cybersecurity Exam Program](https://www.csbs.org/nonbank-cybersecurity-exam-programs-0)** (v1.1, latest release Oct 21, 2024) — for smaller institutions like YS Cap; the **Enhanced** program adds depth for larger/troubled institutions ([CSBS release](https://www.csbs.org/newsroom/csbs-releases-nonbank-cybersecurity-exam-procedures); [Baseline PDF](https://www.csbs.org/sites/default/files/2023-06/Baseline%20Nonbank%20Exam%20Program%20V1.1%20-%20FINAL-6-30-2023.pdf); [Mayer Brown summary](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2022/10/cybersecurity-examination-guidance-for-nonbank-financial-services-companies)).
- Programs are organized around **URSIT** (Uniform Rating System for Information Technology): **Audit, Management, Development & Acquisition, Support & Delivery**. A new third-party data integration lands squarely in *Development & Acquisition* and *Support & Delivery*.
- The CSBS **Document Request List** tells you what examiners will ask for: written information security program, **risk assessment(s)**, board/management cyber reporting, **vendor management documentation**, vulnerability/patching evidence ([CSBS industry overview PDF](https://www.csbs.org/sites/default/files/2022-08/Nonbank%20Exam%20Programs%20Summary%20-%20Industry.pdf)). **Expect to produce, for this integration: the design doc showing read-only architecture, the field inventory, the ICE contract/terms, access-control matrix, and audit-log samples.**
- **If licensed in New York:** [23 NYCRR Part 500](https://www.dfs.ny.gov/system/files/documents/2019/02/dfsrf500txt.pdf) applies to DFS licensees, including mortgage lenders. Notable: **§500.06 audit trail** — systems able to reconstruct material financial transactions, records kept **≥5 years** (3 years for certain logs) ([Cornell LII text](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-york/23-NYCRR-500.6)); 72-hour DFS breach notification; MFA; encryption of NPI; annual certification. Even if YS Cap is not NY-licensed, NYDFS 500 is the de-facto reference bar — and the portal already name-checks it (`src/lib/crypto.js:167`, password policy comment "hardened for a regulated lender… NYDFS 23 NYCRR 500").
- **Record-retention statutes vary by state** and set the floor for loan-file retention (examples from state guides: Montana 5 years from last activity; Maryland 61 months; Ohio 4 years; California brokers 3 years — see [Montana guide](https://doa.mt.gov/BFID/mortgage-consumer-finance/MLguide), [Md. COMAR 09.03.06.05](https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/maryland/COMAR-09-03-06-05), [Ohio 1301:8-2-04](https://codes.ohio.gov/ohio-administrative-code/rule-1301:8-2-04), [CA DRE manual](https://dre.ca.gov/files/pdf/re7.pdf)). Action: enumerate YS Cap's licensed states and take the **longest** applicable period as the retention floor for records of decision (§7).

## 5. MISMO relevance (context only — the portal consumes JSON)

- **MISMO v3.4 Reference Model**: the mortgage industry's standard XML data model + Logical Data Dictionary of standard business names/definitions/enumerations ([MISMO v3.4](https://www.mismo.org/standards-resources/mismo-product/mismo-version-3-4)).
- **ULAD** (Uniform Loan Application Dataset) maps every URLA/1003 field to MISMO v3.4 data points ([Fannie Mae ULAD FAQ](https://singlefamily.fanniemae.com/learning-center/delivering/faqs-uniform-residential-loan-application-uniform-loan-application-dataset); [Freddie Mac ULAD](https://sf.freddiemac.com/tools-learning/uniform-mortgage-data-program/ulad)). **ULDD** (GSE loan delivery, MISMO v3.0) and **UCD** (closing data, MISMO v3.3) are the delivery-side datasets ([Fannie Mae ULDD](https://singlefamily.fanniemae.com/delivering/uniform-mortgage-data-program/uniform-loan-delivery-dataset); [Freddie Mac ULDD](https://sf.freddiemac.com/tools-learning/uniform-mortgage-data-program/uldd)).
- **Why it matters even though Developer Connect returns JSON:**
  1. Encompass's loan object and canonical field names are built to feed these datasets; MISMO's LDD is the best neutral dictionary when writing the **portal↔Encompass field-mapping document** (the data-inventory artifact examiners ask for; the repo already maintains an analogous `docs/FIELD-MAP.md` for ClickUp).
  2. As a business-purpose lender YS Cap doesn't deliver to GSEs, but **whole-loan buyers, warehouse lines, and securitizers of RTL/DSCR product increasingly request MISMO-shaped data tapes** — naming portal columns after MISMO terms is cheap future-proofing.
  3. MISMO terms give a stable vocabulary for the "portal condition cannot clear unless Encompass agrees" rule engine (e.g., milestone/status/condition entities), reducing bespoke-name drift.
- No compliance obligation flows from MISMO itself — it is a standards body, not a regulator.

## 6. SSN/DOB minimization — best practice and current repo posture

**Norms** ([NIST SP 800-122](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/Legacy/SP/nistspecialpublication800-122.pdf)):

- Minimize collection/retention of PII to what is strictly necessary; breach harm scales with what you hold.
- Don't store an SSN used only for verification/matching; note that even **last-4 / partial SSNs are still PII** (linkable near-unique identifiers) and deserve protection, just at lower impact.
- Use one-way keyed hashes for matching, full encryption + access gating + audit for any stored full value.

**Current repo posture is already strong — reuse it, don't reinvent:**

| Control | Where |
|---|---|
| SSN stored only AES-256-GCM encrypted (`bytea`), never plaintext; display column is `ssn_last4` only | `db/schema.sql:57-58` |
| Single write chokepoint normalizing + encrypting SSNs (`ssnForStorage`) | `src/lib/crypto.js:127-139` |
| Identity matching via **HMAC-SHA256 hash** (`ssn_hash` with dedicated `SSN_MATCH_KEY`), not raw SSN | `db/044_borrower_history_backfill.sql:10-12` |
| SSN decryption/viewing is an audited event (`view_ssn`, category `pii`) | `src/lib/audit-actions.js:18-21`, `db/schema.sql:322-335` |
| Chat-layer PII guard: borrower SSN sends blocked, staff sends redacted to last-4 before persistence | `src/lib/pii-guard.js:1-60` |
| External-sync snapshots mask SSN/card, never cleartext | `db/046_clickup_snapshot_audit.sql:6`, `db/110_sync_review_two_sided.sql:9` |

**Recommendation for the Encompass pull (the single biggest governance decision):**

- **Do not ingest full SSN or full DOB from Encompass in Phase 1 at all.** Match loans/borrowers on **Encompass loan GUID + loan number + name/email**, which the pull needs anyway; the portal already has its own SSN capture path with the chokepoint above for the cases where it independently needs one.
- If cross-system identity matching genuinely requires SSN, pull it **transiently in memory, compute the existing `ssn_hash` HMAC, store only the hash + last4, and discard the plaintext** — never persist a second encrypted SSN copy sourced from Encompass (two ciphertexts under two lifecycles = double breach surface and reconciliation drift).
- DOB: if needed for display/verification, prefer **year-only or age**, or store it under the same encryption envelope; exclude it from logs, exports, webhooks, and LLM/analytics payloads.
- Configure minimization **server-side in Encompass too**: API responses honor persona/field-level security — "Persona Access to Field" rules hide denied field IDs from API responses ([Developer Connect 25.3 changelog](https://developer.icemortgagetechnology.com/developer-connect/changelog/253-major-release)), and the API user's persona strictly bounds what the API can see ([Proof: Set Up API Users in Encompass](https://support.proof.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500003014402-Set-Up-API-Users-in-Encompass)). A read-only, field-restricted persona on the dedicated API user means the SSN never even leaves Encompass — defense in depth for the "architecturally incapable" goal.
- Prefer the **`fieldReader` / bounded field-list endpoints** (e.g., `POST /encompass/v3/loans/{loanId}/fieldReader`, per the local Postman index `research/postman-endpoint-index.md`) over `view=full` loan pulls, so the integration requests an explicit allowlist of fields instead of the whole loan object.

## 7. Retention and deletion norms

There is no single "GLBA retention period" — retention is a lattice of floors (keep at least…) and a ceiling norm (dispose when no longer needed):

| Source | Rule of thumb |
|---|---|
| **FTC Safeguards Rule** §314.4(c)(6) | **Dispose within 2 years after last use** to serve the customer, *unless* law or legitimate business need requires longer ([FTC guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/ftc-safeguards-rule-what-your-business-needs-know)) — i.e., a documented retention schedule is itself the compliance artifact |
| **ECOA / Reg B** [12 CFR 1002.12](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-12/chapter-X/part-1002/subpart-A/section-1002.12) | Application + evaluation records: **25 months (consumer) / 12 months (business credit)** after action-taken notice; longer for >$1M-revenue business applicants only on request; **retain until final disposition if under investigation** |
| **State licensing laws** | ~**3-5 years** typical for complete loan files (see §4 examples); take the longest among licensed states |
| **NYDFS 500.06** (if applicable) | Transaction-reconstruction records **≥5 years**; security event logs ≥3 years |
| **State breach laws / litigation** | Legal-hold override: suspend deletion on notice of investigation/litigation |

**Norms applied to the Encompass mirror specifically:**

- **Encompass remains the system of record.** The portal's Encompass-sourced tables are a **derived cache** — re-pullable at any time. Industry norm for derived caches: keep them **thin and short-lived** (retain current-state + enough history for the audit trail of decisions), and let the system of record carry the statutory retention burden. This *also* matters contractually (§8: data-replication restriction).
- What the portal must durably retain is **evidence of its own decisions**: "condition X cleared at time T because Encompass snapshot S showed Y" — i.e., decision-time snapshots of the *specific fields relied upon*, in the audit log, kept for the loan-file retention period. That is distinct from mirroring the whole loan object indefinitely.
- The repo already documents an audit-trail-based retention posture for SharePoint mirrors, citing SEC 17a-4-style audit-trail alternatives (`docs/SHAREPOINT-SECURITY-COMPLIANCE.md:36-53`). **Caution:** that doc's "**GLBA ≥ 6 years**" line overstates — GLBA imposes no general 6-year retention; the FTC rule actually pushes the *opposite* (2-year disposal absent need). Don't copy that number into the Encompass policy; derive the schedule from state licensing floors + Reg B + business need.
- The portal already has soft-delete → purge machinery with audited `purge_application` (`src/lib/audit-actions.js:43`, `db/018_application_soft_delete.sql`) — extend the same pattern to Encompass-sourced rows.

## 8. ICE vendor-risk / API terms of use

Retrieved and read the public **[Encompass Partner Connect API Terms and Conditions (Rev 09-2018)](https://mortgagetech.ice.com/publicdocs/mortgage/encompass-partner-connect-api-terms-and-conditions.pdf)** (3 pp.; local extract at `scratchpad/epc-terms.txt`). Key provisions (quotes verbatim):

- **Credentials:** access tokens/certs must be used "solely for the Permitted Purpose and not share[d] … with any third party" (except authorized contractors under the same restrictions, with the partner "solely liable" for them) — Art. II §2.1.
- **Monitoring/enforcement:** "Ellie Mae has the right to monitor Partner's use of access tokens … to ensure that Partner is complying" (§2.1, §3.4); remedies include warning, **firewall-blocking API calls, and throttling** (§3.6).
- **⚠ Data replication restriction — the headline clause for this project.** §3.1(k): without prior written approval, partner must not "use the API Technology **for the purposes of data replication (e.g., data warehouses or analytics repositories)**." Also restricted: integrations not identified in the order form (3.1(a)), bypassing the Platform Service (3.1(b)), disabling security features (3.1(j)).
- **Termination:** return/destroy API technology and cease integrations (Art. IV).

**Interpretation and caveats (needs counsel + the actual signed contract):**

- These Rev-2018 T&Cs are the **ISV/Partner Connect** terms. YS Cap operating on its **own instance (BE11397907) via Developer Connect** is governed by its own Encompass master agreement/order form (the ICE [Agreements and Terms of Use page](https://mortgagetech.ice.com/agreements) lists the current family of documents — page blocks automated fetch, HTTP 403; review manually). Comparable data-use/replication language commonly appears in the lender-side agreement.
- **Design consequence:** frame and build the portal store as a **bounded operational cache of specific fields needed for defined workflows** (condition gating, CTC gating, borrower display) — *not* a wholesale replica or analytics warehouse of the Encompass database. Keep the pulled-field list short and documented; this simultaneously satisfies the ICE-terms risk, GLBA data-inventory expectations, and minimization (§6).
- Vendor-risk file for the exam (§4): keep the signed ICE agreement + these T&Cs, ICE's security attestations (SOC reports available through ICE account management), the API-user/persona configuration screenshot showing read-only scope, and a periodic reassessment note — that's exactly the §314.4(f) service-provider oversight package.
- Authentication model (for the record): OAuth2 client-credentials for a dedicated **API user whose Encompass persona bounds all API access** ([Developer Connect docs](https://developer.icemortgagetechnology.com/developer-connect/docs/welcome); [Proof setup guide](https://support.proof.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500003014402-Set-Up-API-Users-in-Encompass)). Never make a human's credentials the integration identity.

## 9. Breach notification basics

- **All 50 states** (plus DC/territories) have breach-notification statutes ([NCSL survey](https://www.ncsl.org/technology-and-communication/security-breach-notification-laws); [Privacy Rights Clearinghouse 50-state survey, 2026](https://privacyrights.org/resources-tools/reports/data-breach-notification-laws-50-state-survey-2026-edition)).
- **Trigger data** ("personal information") in most states: first name/initial + last name **plus** SSN, driver's license/state ID number, or financial account number with access credentials — exactly the fields in a loan file. Loan purpose is irrelevant; the guarantor is a resident individual.
- **Timing:** ~20 states have fixed deadlines (30-60 days); the rest require notice "without unreasonable delay"; some require AG/regulator notice above thresholds ([Justia 50-state survey](https://www.justia.com/consumer/identity-theft/security-breach-notification-laws-50-state-survey/)).
- **Encryption safe harbor:** most statutes exempt breaches of **encrypted** data where the key wasn't compromised — the strongest single argument for envelope-encrypting Encompass-sourced sensitive fields and keeping keys out of the database ([Jackson Lewis overview](https://www.jacksonlewis.com/insights/state-data-breach-notification-laws-overview-patchwork); Ohio-style safe harbor for written cyber programs also noted).
- Federal layer: FTC Safeguards 30-day/500-consumer notice (§2.2). If NY-licensed: NYDFS 72-hour event notice.
- The §314.4(h) **incident response plan** should pre-stage: what counts as a notification event for the Encompass store, who calls counsel/insurer, the ICE contact path for a credential compromise (revoke API key/rotate secret in Encompass admin), and log-preservation steps.

Also relevant on the privacy side: **state comprehensive privacy laws**. California's CCPA/CPRA has only a **data-level** GLBA exemption (NPI collected subject to GLBA is exempt from most CCPA duties, but the **breach private right of action still applies**, and non-GLBA-context data isn't exempt); Virginia/Colorado/Utah/Connecticut-style laws have broader **entity-level** exemptions for GLBA financial institutions, though a few newer laws have dropped entity-level exemptions ([GT analysis](https://www.gtlaw-dataprivacydish.com/2021/07/financial-institution-confusion-are-financial-institutions-fully-exempt-from-the-ccpa-cpra-vcdpa-and-cpa/); [Orrick 2025 update](https://www.orrick.com/en/Insights/2025/07/Where-is-the-GLBA-Entity-Level-Exemption-Two-More-State-Privacy-Laws)).

## 10. Audit trail expectations

What examiners and the rules concretely expect, mapped to the integration:

1. **Who accessed what NPI, when** — Safeguards §314.4(c)(8) requires logging authorized-user activity and detecting unauthorized access. The portal's `audit_log` (actor, action, entity, ip, user_agent, jsonb detail — `db/schema.sql:322-335`) is the right shape. **Add integration-specific actions** (mirroring the existing ClickUp-sync pattern, `db/107_clickup_write_journal.sql`): `encompass_pull`, `encompass_view_loan`, `encompass_condition_gate_pass/fail`, `encompass_ctc_gate_pass/fail`, config changes, credential rotation.
2. **Decision reconstruction** — NYDFS 500.06's standard ("reconstruct material financial transactions") is the norm to aim at: every portal decision gated on Encompass data should store the **decision-time snapshot of the exact Encompass fields relied on** (field id, value, Encompass last-modified time, pull timestamp) so a later dispute ("why did the portal clear this condition?") is answerable years later. Retain ≥5 years (NYDFS number; also comfortably covers state loan-file floors).
3. **Integration health/audit of the pipe itself** — log every API call batch (endpoint, loan GUIDs touched, HTTP status, record counts), token issuance, and failures; alert on anomalies (volume spikes = possible credential misuse; ICE monitors its side too, per EPC §3.4).
4. **Encompass-side trails are pullable read-only** and can enrich the portal's evidence: milestone logs (`GET /encompass/v3/loans/{id}/milestones`), disclosure tracking logs (`?entities=disclosureTracking2015Logs`), document audits — all present in the local Postman index (`research/postman-endpoint-index.md`).
5. **Immutability**: audit rows should be append-only (no UPDATE/DELETE grants for the app role beyond insert); the SEC-17a-4-style "audit-trail alternative" reasoning already documented in `docs/SHAREPOINT-SECURITY-COMPLIANCE.md:41-46` applies here too.

## 12. Practical data governance checklist for the Encompass pull

### A. What to store (allowlist — keep it minimal and documented)
- [ ] Encompass **loan GUID, loan number**, folder, milestone/status, **clear-to-close flag**, condition list (id, title, status, cleared date/by), key dates, loan amount/rate/term, property address, borrower/guarantor **name + email + phone**, assigned personnel — i.e., the fields the condition-gating and CTC-gating rules and borrower display actually read.
- [ ] For every gated decision: an **immutable decision-time snapshot** of the exact fields relied on (audit_log jsonb detail).
- [ ] A signed-off, versioned **field-mapping/inventory doc** (MISMO/ULAD names as the neutral vocabulary), reviewed at each field addition — this is the GLBA data-inventory artifact and the ICE "bounded integration" evidence.

### B. What to exclude / minimize
- [ ] **No full SSN** pulled or persisted from Encompass; identity-match via existing `ssn_hash` HMAC (transient compute, discard plaintext) or via loan GUID/loan number. No second encrypted-SSN copy.
- [ ] **No full DOB** unless a concrete workflow requires it; prefer age/year; if stored, same AES-256-GCM envelope as SSN.
- [ ] No credit report payloads, bank account numbers, card numbers, or income-document images in the mirror; documents stay in Encompass/eFolder (fetch on demand, don't warehouse).
- [ ] Enforce exclusions **server-side too**: dedicated API user with a read-only, field-restricted persona ("Persona Access to Field" denies SSN etc. from ever appearing in API responses); prefer `fieldReader` allowlist calls over `view=full`.
- [ ] Strip sensitive fields from logs, error messages, webhooks, exports, and any analytics/LLM payloads (extend `src/lib/redact.js` / `pii-guard` scanning to the sync path, as ClickUp sync already does — `db/107_clickup_write_journal.sql:9`).

### C. Encryption & credentials
- [ ] TLS for all API traffic (default); **at-rest encryption** for the Postgres instance plus **field-level AES-256-GGM envelope** for any sensitive field mirrored (reuses `src/lib/crypto.js`); keys in env/secret manager, never in DB; document key rotation.
- [ ] OAuth client secret / API key for the Encompass API user stored in the secret manager, rotated on schedule and on personnel change; never in code or logs; scoped to instance BE11397907.
- [ ] Encryption posture documented → preserves state-law and FTC **encryption safe harbor**.

### D. Access control
- [ ] Encompass side: dedicated **API-only user**, read-only persona, field-level denies, org-hierarchy scoping; no human logins on that account.
- [ ] Portal side: Encompass-sourced data visible only per existing role/permission system (`db/039_roles_permissions.sql`); borrower sees only their own file; staff MFA required (already implemented).
- [ ] Quarterly access review of both the persona and portal roles (Safeguards "regularly reconsider business necessity").

### E. Audit
- [ ] Append-only `audit_log` actions for every pull batch, every staff view of Encompass-sourced sensitive data, every gate decision (pass/fail with field snapshot), config/credential changes.
- [ ] Pull-job telemetry: endpoint, loans touched, counts, status, latency; anomaly alerts (volume/off-hours spikes).
- [ ] Retain audit records **≥5 years**; sample them into the annual QI report and the exam binder.

### F. Retention & deletion
- [ ] Written retention schedule: **operational cache** rows refreshed/pruned continuously (cache, not archive); **decision evidence + loan-file records** retained to the longest applicable state floor (typically 3-5 yrs; 25/12-month Reg B minimums subsumed); **dispose within 2 years of last use** anything with no documented need (FTC §314.4(c)(6)).
- [ ] Deletion is audited (`purge_*` actions) and suspended under legal hold; deletion of a borrower cascades per schedule, not ad hoc.
- [ ] Do **not** import the "GLBA ≥ 6 years" figure from `docs/SHAREPOINT-SECURITY-COMPLIANCE.md:38` — derive the schedule from actual state floors + business need.
